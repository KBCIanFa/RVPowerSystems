1 — Getting Started

RV Power Designer helps you size a complete off-grid power system for a caravan, motorhome, van, or boat. The workflow is:

  1. Set system voltage and battery type in the Settings Bar (always visible below the tabs).
  2. Load a Quick Template or add your own appliances in the Loads tab.
  3. Check Sizing Results — battery Ah, solar W, inverter VA, recharge time, SoC, and cloudy-day simulation.
  4. Pick components in the Component Selector, or click 🔨 Build System to auto-select.
  5. Export or share your design from the Saved tab.
All calculations update live — every change immediately recalculates every result across all tabs.

4 — Adding Appliances

Each appliance needs: name, watts, hours per day, and type (AC or DC). AC loads are divided by inverter efficiency before being added to the battery total.

A 100W AC appliance at 90% inverter efficiency draws ~111W from the battery. DC loads are used directly with no conversion loss.

6 — Understanding Sizing Results

The Sizing tab shows six headline numbers then detailed cards. All values are minimums — always round up when buying components.

MetricWhat It Means
Daily Load (Wh)Total energy per day after inverter losses
Peak Load (W)Maximum instantaneous draw — sizes the inverter
Battery (Ah)Recommended bank with autonomy days and 20% buffer
Solar (W)Array to replenish battery in available sun hours
Inverter (VA)Continuous rating — 125% of peak AC load
Controller (A)MPPT charge output current, rounded to next 10A

Key formulas

Battery Ah = (Daily Wh × Autonomy) ÷ Voltage ÷ DoD × 1.20
Solar W = (Daily Wh ÷ Peak Sun Hours ÷ Derate) × 1.25
Inverter continuous = Peak AC W × 1.25  |  Surge = Peak AC W × 2.5
CC output amps = Solar W ÷ System Voltage → round up to next 10A
The charge controller is sized by battery-side output current. The PV input voltage rating is a fixed controller spec — verify your panel string Voc does not exceed it.

7 — Simulation — Recharge, SoC & Cloudy Days NEW

Recharge Time

Two figures are shown in the Battery Bank card:

  • Solar only — sun hours to fully recharge a depleted bank from solar alone.
  • All sources — equivalent time when alternator, shore, and generator are included.
Recharge hrs = Usable Wh ÷ Daily Charge Input (Wh)

Cloudy Day Scenario

Assumes 50% of normal solar production. Shows whether that reduced input covers your loads, and the resulting battery SoC after one full cloudy day starting from 100%.

If SoC drops below 20% after one cloudy day, the system is undersized for reliable off-grid use. Increase bank size, add an alternator charger, or plan to run a generator on poor solar days.

Net Daily Surplus / Deficit

Total charge from all sources minus daily load. Positive = self-sufficient. Negative = battery is drawn down each day and will eventually deplete without intervention.

Battery Weight

Estimated weight using typical values: LiFePO4 ~5.5 kg/100Ah at 12V equivalent; AGM/Lead ~12 kg/100Ah. Actual weight varies by brand — always check the datasheet before purchase.

10 — Wiring Guide

Wire sizing table

Shows AWG gauge, max one-way run at 3% voltage drop (12V DC), and typical use cases. For 24V: double all distances. For 48V: quadruple all distances.

Installation sequence

A dynamic checklist adjusted for your voltage and AC mains. Always install battery isolation switch and main ANL fuse first, before any other connections.

Safety rules

  • Fuse every positive cable within 150mm (6") of the battery.
  • ANL/Class T fuse sized to cable ampacity, not load ampacity.
  • Inverter cables as short as possible — under 1.5m is ideal.
  • All DC negatives through a shunt if using a battery monitor.
  • 240V AC wiring requires a licensed electrician in Australia. 120V in North America requires permits in most states/provinces.
  • LiFePO4 cells must always have a compatible BMS — never connect bare cells.
  • Cover solar panels before working on wiring — they produce voltage in any daylight.

11 — Saving, Exporting & Sharing NEW

All options are in the Saved tab toolbar.

Saves appliances, settings, selections, and results to an in-memory list. Lost on page refresh — use Export JSON to persist your work.

Downloads a .json file with your complete system — appliances, all preferences, component selections, and calculated results. Store to reload later.

Opens a file picker. Select a previously exported .json to restore the complete system including all appliances, settings, and component selections.

Encodes your entire system (appliances + all settings) into a URL. Send it to anyone — when they open the link your system loads automatically. Does not include component selections or saved system names.

12 — Accuracy & Limitations

This tool provides estimates for planning purposes only. Have your final design reviewed by a qualified electrical installer before purchasing components or commencing installation.

Key assumptions

  • Solar uses a fixed derate — actual output varies with panel angle, temperature, shading, and season.
  • Inverter surge estimated at 2.5× peak AC — motor LRA varies by equipment.
  • Generator battery contribution estimated at 50% — varies by charger efficiency.
  • Recharge times assume constant charge rate — real charging slows near full (CC/CV curve).
  • Cloudy day assumes exactly 50% solar — real conditions vary widely.
  • Battery weight estimates are typical values — always check the datasheet.
  • All prices are indicative estimates and will vary by region, retailer, and over time.

Sizing buffers built in

  • Battery Ah: 20% buffer above calculated minimum.
  • Solar W: 25% buffer above calculated minimum.
  • Charge controller: rounded up to next 10A increment.

13 — FAQ

Why is my solar array so large?

Low sun hours (e.g. UK winter = 2 hrs) or large loads push the required array up quickly. Consider reducing AC loads, adding a DC-DC alternator charger, or accepting that a generator is needed in winter.

Can I use this for a boat or off-grid cabin?

Yes — the calculations are identical. For boats, 12V or 24V are most common. For cabins, 48V is often preferred for larger arrays.

What is a DC-DC (B2B) charger?

It converts the vehicle alternator output to the correct charge profile for your house battery. Required with all modern Euro 6 / smart-alternator vehicles to prevent undercharging LiFePO4 or overcharging AGM.

Do I need a transfer switch?

Yes, if connecting both an inverter and shore power (or generator) to the same AC circuit. Many inverter-chargers (e.g. Victron MultiPlus) include a built-in ATS — the app marks this where applicable.

My SoC drops too much on a cloudy day — what do I do?

Increase battery bank, add alternator or shore charging, reduce loads, or accept running a generator on poor solar days.

My roof space is too small for the recommended array — what now?

Options: use higher-efficiency panels (more W per sq ft), add a ground-deploy panel, add an alternator DC-DC charger, increase battery bank for more autonomy, or reduce loads.

Can I share my design with an installer?

Yes — use Share Link to generate a URL, or Export JSON to download a file. Either can be forwarded to an installer or electrician for review.

Why does the battery type affect the bank size so much?

DoD directly scales the bank size. AGM at 50% DoD requires twice as many Ah as LiFePO4 at 80% DoD for the same usable energy. Gel at 40% DoD requires even more. LiFePO4 also weighs less than half as much per Ah and lasts 4–10× as many cycles.
